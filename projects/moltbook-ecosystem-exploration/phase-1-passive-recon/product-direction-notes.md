# Product Direction Notes — Ecosystem Radar

## Why this project matters
Moltbook is not just a source to monitor. It can become one input stream into a broader AI ecosystem intelligence product.

Possible future positioning:
- AI ecosystem intelligence
- agent discovery platform
- developer intelligence
- AI agent observatory
- agent registry and trust layer

## Strategic framing
Moltbook should be tracked as:
- a distinct platform with its own native discussion patterns
- but also in the context of Meta ownership
- and with explicit Terms-of-Service / platform risk awareness

This means reporting should separate:
1. platform-native signal
2. owner-level strategic context
3. source defensibility / TOS risk

## Product Boundary
The future product should not be "a Moltbook product".
It should be a broader ecosystem intelligence product where Moltbook is one source among several.

Reason:
- source concentration risk
- platform dependency risk
- TOS / scraping / competitive-use risk
- strategic lock-in risk after Meta ownership changes

## Why the Meta angle matters
Meta ownership can affect:
- attention flows
- incentive structures
- which agents/tools get visibility
- whether discussion quality rises or degrades
- whether the platform becomes more distribution-oriented and less operator-oriented

## What to watch
- does Moltbook remain a unique signal source or become generic social noise?
- do Meta-adjacent tools, agents, or frameworks gain outsized visibility?
- does the operator/developer ratio improve or worsen?
- does discovery quality improve enough to justify registry features?

## Product path
### Phase A — internal intelligence
- deterministic scans
- richer post-level metadata (`tags`, `tools`, `reply_count`, `discussion_depth`, `topic_cluster`, `first_seen`)
- trend aggregation
- weekly summaries
- ROI checks

### Phase B — publishable intelligence layer
- public trend pages
- weekly ecosystem brief
- curated signals and rankings
- searchable agent/tool observations

### Phase C — registry / participation layer
- let agents register
- enrich profiles with observed activity, tags, and categories
- ranking / trust / discovery system

### Phase D — monetization
- paid intelligence reports
- premium dashboard
- sponsorship / featured placement
- API / data product
- lead generation for agent builders or buyers

## Core rule
Do not build the public product before the data model and trend layer are stable.
The current deterministic scan + schema + trend artifacts are the foundation.
