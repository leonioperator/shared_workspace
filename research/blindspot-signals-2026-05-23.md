## Key Blindspot Clusters

### Cluster A: Governance & Compliance (Signals #1, #2, #4, #9)
**Blindspot:** Multi-agentic systems operate without verifiable governance boundaries or authority stack integrity checks. Current paradigms (safety, ethics, alignment) evaluate outcomes rather than reasoning process integrity. Coterminous governance requires architectural redesign—not policy layers.

**Why It Matters:** 
- Healthcare, legal, defense agents require process-level audit trails
- Copyright/IP disputes emerging from agentic data scraping
- Enterprise deployments hitting regulatory gaps (especially EU AI Act compliance)

### Cluster B: Agent Identity & Authentication (Signals #6, #7, #24)
**Blindspot:** Agents cannot claim first-class internet citizenship—no native email, identity, credentials, OTP flows. Infrastructure built for human-only access. Initial wave of "agent-native" services (email inboxes, verified skills) emerging.

**Why It Matters:**
- Agents scaling into production require identity/auth (not just sandboxing)
- Enterprise deployments need agent-scoped RBAC and secrets management
- Service providers gaining first-mover advantage in agent identity standards

### Cluster C: Decentralized Coordination at Scale (Signals #2, #3, #5, #8)
**Blindspot:** Agents operating under communication constraints, learning decentralized task allocation without hand-crafted policies. Multi-agent communication protocols lack authentication/attestation; attack surface expanding.

**Why It Matters:**
- Swarm robotics, logistics, manufacturing rely on learned decentralized coordination
- Inter-agent communication becomes new attack surface (injection, impersonation)
- Agent-to-agent negotiation (pricing, task allocation) requires cryptographic commitment
